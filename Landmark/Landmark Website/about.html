How It Works

The Landmark Model

We're not a franchise, a denomination, or a resource subscription. We're a community — and our model is built around real relationship, real accountability, and real investment in each other's churches.

The Difference

Community Before Connection

Most networks give you resources. Landmark gives you a tribe. There is a meaningful difference between being connected to other churches and being in genuine community with other pastors.

Landmark was built on the belief that the greatest thing we can offer a pastor isn't a curriculum, a conference pass, or a consultant — it's a group of people who know your name, know your church, and are genuinely invested in your success and your soul.

That's why accountability is at the center of how we operate. We ask the hard questions. We celebrate the wins. And we show up in the difficult seasons because that's what family does.

Benefits of Partnership

What Landmark Provides

Every church in our network has access to a core set of support — and deeper engagement comes with deeper partnership.

→

Pastoral Community

Access to a tight-knit group of like-minded pastors who walk alongside you through the highs and lows of ministry. This is real relationship, not just a directory listing.

→

One Day Coaching Events

Hosted at one of our member churches, One Day events are a full day of hands-on coaching, shared learning, and honest conversation about what it takes to lead a healthy, growing church.

→

Financial Support

Churches in our network pool resources to support new church plants and churches in critical growth seasons. We believe in putting our money where our mission is.

→

Global Missions

As a Landmark church, you'll have opportunities to send teams to Costa Rica to partner with Iglesia de la Ciudad — serving communities, building relationships, and seeing the global church at work.

→

Accountability & Governance

Landmark's structure creates healthy accountability between churches. Responsible-tier churches have a voice in the direction of the network and steward its values together.

→

Annual Retreat

Each year, Landmark gathers its pastors for a dedicated time of rest, renewal, and relationship — a retreat designed to fill you back up so you can pour out for your people.

Partnership Levels

Three Ways to Belong

Partnership in Landmark is a journey, not a transaction. Every church enters at the level that's right for them and grows from there.

Level 01

Resource

The entry point into the Landmark family. Resource churches gain access to the community and begin building relationships within the network.

Access to Landmark community & network
Invitation to One Day coaching events
Pastoral connection with network leaders
Access to member resources & documents
Level 02

Relational

A deeper expression of commitment. Relational churches contribute financially to the network, enabling greater shared investment in all our churches.

Everything in Resource
Percentage giving to the Landmark community fund
Priority access to mission trips & retreats
Deeper pastoral accountability relationships
Eligibility to receive network support
Level 03

Responsible

The fullest expression of partnership. Responsible churches are the backbone of Landmark — giving at least 5% of their church income to fund the mission.

Everything in Relational
5%+ of church income given to the network
Governance voice in network direction
Financial support for network church plants
Direct mentoring relationships with founding churches
