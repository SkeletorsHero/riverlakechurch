This could be a chapter on that concept but what does it look like to train yourself vs your alligator.  Every time we feed it we are training it to come back, to get bigger, to get stronger.  But then with ourselves we simply try and then fail.  No wonder our addictions/emotions/struggles have so much power over us. We’ve spent time training our issues and just trying to get better.

This has nothing to do with heart, love of God, or desires.  There are a lot of Christians who are stuck in patterns that are harmful to them.


One of the most important aspects of training is you know you won’t get it right the first time.  There is this expectation that you are not great at this and so failure, while not enjoyed, is expected.  When you try it feels like either pass or fail but when you train the goal is simply to get better over time.  You can still fail but the process is longer, better, and allows us to grow into who God has called us to be.  We need to expect to fail in the beginning.  

Expectations determine our experience…high ones (trying) will help us fall.  Low ones help us rise to the occasion.
