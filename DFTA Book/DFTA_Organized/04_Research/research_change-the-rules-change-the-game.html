In the winter of 1891 the game of basketball was invented.  Not because Dr. James Naismith was looking to invent a new sport, but because the boys he was responsible for were tired of playing football in the gym because of a blizzard that had hit the town.  He divided them up into teams of 9, put up two peach baskets, one on each end, gave them an old soccer ball and told them the goal was put the ball into the other teams peach basket.  

Can you guess what happened next?  The boys began tackling, kicking, punching inside the scrum that had developed after the ball.  The result was one boy was knocked out, several had black eyes, and one had a dislocated shoulder.  

Can you guess what Dr. Naismith did?  He didn’t do what I would have done.  I would have yelled at the boys.  I would have assumed they weren’t good enough to play the game.  I would have went and found different people to play the game or decided that the game itself was a bad idea.  

Luckily, for those boys and the game of basketball it wasn’t me.  He simply changed a few rules, one of which was no running with the ball.  This stopped the need for tackling.  The next game they didn’t have one casualty!  And a fun sport was invented that people play all over the world today.
